Welcome to Taniti

Discover a tropical paradise in the Pacific. With breathtaking beaches, lush rainforests, and a rich local culture, Taniti is the perfect getaway for relaxation and adventure.

Varied Terrain

Though less than 500 square miles, our island features sandy and rocky beaches, a safe harbor, lush tropical rainforests, and a mountainous interior complete with an active volcano.

Rich Culture

Home to an indigenous population of about 20,000, Taniti offers a warm, welcoming environment. Explore native architecture and a history rooted in fishing and agriculture.

Yellow Leaf Bay

The bustling heart of our coastal life. Enjoy white, sandy beaches, modern amenities, and the rapidly developing Merriton Landing area on the north side of the bay.

Island Amenities

Groceries & Daily Needs

Whether you're cooking at your rental or need a midnight snack, Taniti has you covered.

  • Supermarkets: 2 large stores for all major grocery needs.
  • Local Grocers: 2 smaller grocery stores with fresh daily provisions.
  • Convenience: 1 convenience store open 24 hours a day, 7 days a week.

Healthcare Services

Your safety and health are important to us.

  • Hospital: One central hospital equipped with modern facilities. The hospital employs many multilingual staff members to assist tourists.
  • Clinics: Several clinics located across the island for minor issues and check-ups.

Banking & Currency

Taniti's official currency is the U.S. Dollar (USD).

  • Many businesses also accept Euros and Yen.
  • Major credit cards are widely accepted across the island.
  • Several local banks are available to facilitate currency exchange.

Transportation

Getting to Taniti

By Air

Almost all visitors arrive by air. Our small, modern airport accommodates small jets and propeller planes. We are currently expanding the airport to allow larger jets to land within the next few years!

By Sea

A small cruise ship docks in beautiful Yellow Leaf Bay for one night per week, offering a scenic maritime arrival.

Getting Around the Island

Taniti City is fairly flat and very walkable, especially around the Merriton Landing area where many tourists stay. For exploring further, you have several options:

Buses

Public buses serve Taniti City (5am - 11pm daily). Private buses serve the rest of the island.

Taxis

Readily available throughout Taniti City for quick and direct transportation.

Rental Cars

Available to rent from a local rental agency conveniently located near the airport.

Bicycles

Available from several vendors. Note: Helmets are required by law.

Lodging & Hotels

Peace of mind: All types of lodging on Taniti are strictly regulated and regularly inspected by the Tanitian government to ensure quality and safety.

Four-Star Resort

Experience ultimate luxury at our premier large resort. Enjoy world-class amenities, stunning ocean views, and premium services.

Family-Owned Hotels

Immerse yourself in local culture by staying in one of our many cozy, family-owned hotels scattered around the island.

Bed and Breakfasts

Enjoy a quiet, intimate stay at a growing number of charming B&Bs, perfect for romantic getaways.

Budget Hostels

Traveling on a budget? Our inexpensive hostel provides clean, safe, and friendly accommodations for backpackers and solo travelers.

Entertainment & Activities

New: 9-hole Golf Course opening next year!

Nature & Sightseeing

Most tourists spend their time in Taniti City, taking in the native architecture and the beautiful white sandy beaches that encircle Yellow Leaf Bay. For the adventurers:

  • ✔️ Rainforest hikes & Zip-lining
  • ✔️ Active Volcano visits

City Life (Merriton Landing)

The rapidly developing area of Merriton Landing on the north side of Yellow Leaf Bay is the hub for indoor and evening entertainment.

Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to the most common questions from our visitors to help you plan your perfect trip.

Power outlets are 120 volts, which is the exact same standard used in the United States. US travelers will not need a voltage converter.

The drinking age on Taniti is 18 (though it is not strictly enforced). Please note that by law, alcohol is not allowed to be served or sold between the hours of midnight and 9:00 a.m.

Yes, many younger Tanitians speak fluent English. However, very little English is spoken in the rural areas, especially by the older residents.

Yes, violent crime is very rare on Taniti. However, as tourism has increased, there have been more reports of pickpocketing and other petty crimes. We advise visitors to keep their valuables secure, especially in crowded tourist areas.

Taniti enjoys a large number of national holidays. Visitors should plan accordingly, as many tourist attractions and restaurants will be closed on these days. Check local calendars before planning specific excursions!
